## How to use this audit

1. **Browse the fix backlog** at [docs/ds-audit-fix-backlog.md](ds-audit-fix-backlog.md). Sessions are ordered by leverage â€” top sessions unblock the most pages per hour of work.
2. **Run `/ds-fix`** to pick up the next `open` session. The command reads the backlog, confirms scope with Jim, executes, verifies via Chrome MCP, marks the session `done`.
3. **Per-pattern or per-page?** Both approaches are valid:
   - **Per-pattern** (recommended start): add a prop to Text/Button/Divider â€” immediately reduces inline styles across 10+ pages. Top 3 sessions = 40+ fixes.
   - **Per-page** (detail-flow focus): take one worst-page at a time (ClientDetailClient first). Better visual verification, but less leverage.
4. **Don't fix everything at once.** The backlog is sized for 1-2 hour sessions; plough through at a cadence that suits.
